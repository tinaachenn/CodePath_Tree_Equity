Tree Equity

Trees are for everyone, everywhere.

What is Tree Equity? Why Does it Matter?

Tree equity is the notion that everyone, regardless of race, socio-economic background, or location, should beable to experience the benefits that trees provide. Trees should be equally distributed and assessible in a community. Trees, in addition to providing health, recreational, and economic benefits, also play a large part in mitigating the effects of climate change. Tree equity in cities and urban environments is especially important as many low income, BIPOC, and underprivileged communitities all around the world suffer from the lack of sufficient access to trees.

This website focuses on the macrocosmic notion of tree equity and its importance before diving into Phoenix, Arizona's microcosmic tree equity stance and condition. The aim is to educate about the power of trees, their importance, and how to reach tree equity. In addition, we hope to connect you to organizations workings towards tree equity who are in need of volunteers and donations. Together, we can give everyone the trees they deserve.
